Chapter 01 — The maestro's bet
—

Greenspan heard a different tune.

Through 1996 and 1997, unemployment kept falling and his colleagues called for higher rates. Greenspan held. He inferred from anecdote, not statistics, that an IT productivity boom was arriving before it showed in the models.

Chapter 02 — The payoff
—

The boom was real.

Productivity growth nearly doubled in the late 1990s compared with the first half of the decade. The economy grew faster than the models said it safely could, exactly as Greenspan had wagered.

Chapter 03 — The dog that didn't bark
—

Inflation never came.

Unemployment sank to levels that were supposed to guarantee rising prices. Instead inflation stayed tame for the rest of the decade. Productivity, it turned out, had changed what the economy could bear.

Chapter 04 — Today's echo
—

The tune is playing again. Maybe.

Productivity growth since 2023 is running near late-1990s levels, and AI is the anecdote in every boardroom. But this time inflation has not settled. Whether the Fed can afford to trust the boom is the open question, and it's yours to explore.
